Arabic

كلمةٌ - Word

Words are broken up into three types

  1. اسم - Nouns:
    • Four characteristics of nouns:
      1. Definite (معرفة) and Indefinite (نكرة)
        • Definite is either a name or has ال preceding it.
        • Indefinite has tanween
      2. I'raab اعراب - The Three States (حال)
        1. رفع
        2. نصب
        3. جرّ
      3. Number
        1. مفرد - Singular
        2. مثنّى - Dual
          • Masculine: Add fatha and انِ
          • Feminine: Remove ة and add تانِ
        3. جمع - Plural
          1. Regular
            • جمع المذكر السالم - Sound Masculine Plural: Add ونَ at the end
            • جمع المؤنث السالم Sound Feminine Plural: اتِ at the end
          2. Irregular جمع التكسير - No set rules
      4. Derived nouns: placing the letters of مصدر (verbal noun/infinitive) in different forms
        1. اسم الفاعل the doer
        2. اسم المفعول the object
        3. الصفة المشبَّ باسم الفاعل adjective for doer
          • Common Patterns:
            1. ٌفعيل (the plurals of these words are usually جمع التكسير )
            2. فعول
            3. فعلان (feminine is on the pattern فعلى)
            4. فعَّال (emphasis- extremely)
        4. اسم تفضيل Comparalative and superlative nouns. Masculine follows: أفعل. Feminine follows: فعلى
  2. Gender
    • By default, words are masculine (المذكر)
    • Words are classified as feminine (المؤنث) if they are naturally feminine or they have ة
  • فعل - Verbs:
    • Characterized by the following five:
      1. Tense
        1. فعل الماضي Past Tense
        2. فعل المضارع Present tense

            3. Ex: يذهب زيدٌ can have three meanings:

          1. Present Habitual - Zayd goes
          2. Present Continuous - Zayd is going
          3. Future - Zayd will go
        3. فعل الأمر Imperative/Command - Ex: اِذهبْGo.
      2. I'raab اعراب - verbs are either مبني or معرب
        • فعل الماضي is always مبني (unchanging)
        • فعل المضارع is معرب (changing) and can be any of the three : رفع or نصب or جزم
        • فعل الأمر is مجزوم
      3. Affirmative and Negative - Verbs are either affirmative or negative
        • Affirmative مثْبَتٌ- shows action took place
        • Negative منفِيٌّ - shows action did not take place. Preceded by
        • Negative Participles include:
          1. لم for مضارع makes verb مجزوم
          2. ما for ماضي and مضارع
          3. لا for مضارع
          4. لن for مضارع makes verb منصوب
      4. Gender - Refer to pronouns chart
      5. Voice - Two types
        1. Active voice فعل معلوم- subject of sentence carries out sentence
        2. Passive voice فعل مجهول - subject of sentence becomes recipient of action
    • Types of Verbs
      1. الفعل الصحيح Sound Verbs
        1. Irregular Sound Verbs
          1. الفعل المضَعَّف Doubled verbs- two root letters are the same (Ex: to reply يرُدُّ ردَّ)
          2. الفعل المهموز - verbs with ء as a consonant (Ex: to begin يبدَع بدأ)
        2. فعل صحيح سالم Regular Sound Verbs
      2. الفعل المُعتَل Weak Verbs
        1. فعل لفيف has two weak letters (Ex: وفى)
        2. فعل مثال Assimilated verbs - Begins with ي or و (Ex: to put يضع وضع)
        3. فعل أجوف Hollow verbs - ي or و in the middle (Ex: to sell يبيع باع)
        4. فعل ناقص ends with ي or و (Ex: to throw رمى يرمي)
  • حرف - Particles: used to give additional meaning to noun/verb.
    • Two Characteristics:
      1. اعراب - huroof are مبني
      2. Governance - whether they change the following word
        1. عامِل changes اعراب of word following it
        2. غير عامل doesn't change اعراب
