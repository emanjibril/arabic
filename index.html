اللُّغَة العربِيَّة

كلِماتٌ - Words are broken into three types

  1. اسم - Nouns:
    • Four characteristics of nouns:
      1. Definite (معرفة) and Indefinite (نكرة)
        • Definite is either a name or has ال preceding it.
        • Indefinite has tanween
      2. I'raab اعراب
        • Nouns Occur in Three States (حال)
          1. رفع: Six
            1. فاعل
              • مسند إليه: the subject of the sentence ie. the sentence depends on it
              • سبع أحكام الفاعل
                1. وجوب رفعه: has to be raf'
                2. Has to be preceded by فعل أو شبه الجملة
                  • If the fa'il comes before, it would be considered a mubtada
                  • شبه الجملة can be إسم الفاعل، مصدر، إسم تفضيل
                  • Example: المجتهد زيدٌ تعبان: x (المجتهد زيد) مبتدأ and تعبان: خبر and المجتهد: شبه الجملة
                3. has to be mentioned directly, hidden (مستتر), or in the context ( دلّ عليه)
                4. In an Question/Answer format, Fa'il may not be present in the answer. Example: Q: A:
                5. فاعل limits the verb to a singular (M/F) state.
                6. The default is فاعل comes before maf'ool
                7. If fi'il is Feminine, فاعل is F. If fi'il is M, فاعل is Masculine
            2. نصب
            3. جرّ
        • Nouns are either مبني or معرب
          • The endings of nouns which are مبني does not change
            1. Demonstrative nouns اسم اشارة (Ex: هذا)
            2. Pronouns ضير
            3. Relative nouns اسم موصول (Ex: اللذي)
            4. Interrogative nouns حروف استفهام (Ex: كم،أين)
            5. Time/place of something happening بعض ظروف (Ex: إذا،أمس)
            6. Expressive nouns اسماء الأفعال (Ex: اف)
          • The endings of nouns which do change and there are six types
            • مفرد, مثنّى, جمع المذكر السالم, جمع المؤنث السالم, جمع التكسير
            • غير منصرف (cannot take tanween or kasrah) - categories:
              1. Non-arabic names
              2. Female names
              3. Names with ة
              4. Names in the pattern of a verb (Ex: أحمد)
              5. Names on the scale فُعَل
              6. Names with ان at the end
      3. Number
        1. مفرد - Singular
        2. مثنّى - Dual
          • Masculine: Add fatha and انِ
          • Feminine: Remove ة and add تانِ
        3. جمع - Plural
          1. Regular
            • جمع المذكر السالم - Sound Masculine Plural: Add ونَ at the end
            • جمع المؤنث السالم Sound Feminine Plural: اتِ at the end
          2. Irregular جمع التكسير - No set rules
      4. Gender
        • By default, words are masculine (المذكر)
        • Words are classified as feminine (المؤنث) if they are naturally feminine or they have ة
    • مشتقٌّ Derived nouns: placing the letters of مصدر (verbal noun/infinitive) in different forms
      1. اسم الفاعل the doer
      2. اسم المفعول the object
      3. الصفة المشبَّهة باسم الفاعل adjective for doer
        • Common Patterns:
          1. ٌفعيل (the plurals of these words are usually جمع التكسير)
          2. فعول
          3. فعلان (feminine is on the pattern فعلى)
          4. فعَّال (emphasis- extremely)
      4. اسم تفضيل Comparative and superlative nouns. Masculine follows: أفعل. Feminine follows: فعلى
  2. فعل - Verbs:
    • Characterized by the following five:
      1. Tense
        1. فعل الماضي Past Tense
        2. فعل المضارع Present tense

          Ex: يذهب زيدٌ can have three meanings:

          1. Present Habitual - Zayd goes
          2. Present Continuous - Zayd is going
          3. Future - Zayd will go
        3. فعل الأمر Imperative/Command - Ex: اِذهبْ Go.
      2. I'raab اعراب - verbs are either مبني or معرب
        • فعل الماضي is always مبني (unchanging)
        • فعل المضارع is معرب (changing) and can be any of the three : رفع or نصب or جزم
        • فعل الأمر is مجزوم
      3. Affirmative and Negative - Verbs are either affirmative or negative
        • Affirmative مثْبَتٌ - shows action took place
        • Negative منفِيٌّ - shows action did not take place. Preceded by
          • Negative Participles include:
            1. لم for مضارع makes verb مجزوم
            2. ما for ماضي and مضارع
            3. لا for مضارع
            4. لن for مضارع makes verb منصوب
      4. Gender - Refer to pronouns chart
      5. Voice - Two types
        1. Active voice فعل معلوم - subject of sentence carries out sentence
        2. Passive voice فعل مجهول - subject of sentence becomes recipient of action
    • Types of Verbs
      1. الفعل الصحيح Sound Verbs
        1. Irregular Sound Verbs
          1. الفعل المضَعَّف Doubled verbs- two root letters are the same (Ex: to reply يرُدُّ ردَّ)
          2. الفعل المهموز - verbs with ء as a consonant (Ex: to begin يبدَع بدأ)
        2. فعل صحيح سالم Regular Sound Verbs
      2. الفعل المُعتَل Weak Verbs
        1. فعل لفيف has two weak letters (Ex: وفى)
        2. فعل مثال Assimilated verbs - Begins with ي or و (Ex: to put يضع وضع)
        3. فعل أجوف Hollow verbs - ي or و in the middle (Ex: to sell يبيع باع)
        4. فعل ناقص ends with ي or و (Ex: to throw رمى يرمي)
  3. حرف - Particles: used to give additional meaning to noun/verb.
    • Two Characteristics:
      1. اعراب - huroof are مبني
      2. Governance - whether they change the following word
        1. عامِل changes اعراب of word following it
        2. غير عامل doesn't change اعراب

جمل - Sentences are broken into two types:Hello

  1. جملة فعلية
    • Sentence begins with a verb, or حرف نفي, and has a فاعل as the subject and مفعول به as the object
    • When there is a حرف نفي, it's associated with whichever word is nakira. For example: ما جاء رجل: no man came vs ما شرب الولد ماء: the boy did not drink any water
